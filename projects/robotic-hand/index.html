Robotic Hand R&D

Exploring automated robotic hand feasibility for factory assembly — from concept to phased validation demos

CompanySomic Ishikawa
RoleR&D Engineer
Year2024 — Present
TypeInternal R&D
🦾

Overview

An internal R&D initiative at Somic Ishikawa to explore the feasibility of an automated robotic hand for factory managers — aimed at improving efficiency by automating repeatable assembly work that currently requires human operators.

The project involved defining real-world usage scenarios, building a prototype, and running phased validation cycles to assess viability for production deployment.

Research Approach

  • Defined two core usage scenarios: standing work (fixed position tasks) and moving work (dynamic assembly tasks)
  • Validated Phase 1 feasibility through successful standing-work cycles (left/right), demonstrating repeatable motion reliability
  • Identified finger-grip limitations during moving tasks — defining clear next-step requirements for the product roadmap
  • Structured findings into a phased development plan for future engineering iterations

Key Achievements

  • Successfully validated first-phase feasibility through repeatable standing-work motion demos
  • Defined a clear product roadmap based on identified technical limitations
  • Bridged gap between research concept and engineering requirements for future development

US–Japan Power Tester Integration

Successfully delivered a startup-built power testing system to Japan factory teams — as the primary EN–JP technical bridge from validation through production acceptance

CompanySomic Ishikawa
RoleR&D Engineer
Year2024 — Present
TypeProduct Validation
PWR

Overview

Partnered with a US startup engineering team and Japan factory teams to bring a Wi-Fi-enabled power testing machine from prototype to successful on-site deployment. As the primary bilingual technical coordinator, I aligned test plans across time zones, diagnosed intermittent zero-data transmission that blocked downstream analytics, and drove a software fix that stabilized data flow — enabling factory operators to use real-time voltage and power analysis in production.

Integration Capabilities

Cross-border delivery from stress-test design through root-cause diagnosis to factory acceptance.

Cross-border EN–JP technical bridge

Primary coordination point between US startup engineers and Japan factory teams — aligning test criteria and closing the loop from failure to acceptance.

Stress-test cycle design

Designed node-scaling stress tests to isolate routing failures and identify limits in the shortest-path algorithm under increasing load.

Zero-data transmission diagnosis

Owned investigation of intermittent zero-data issues that blocked downstream analytics and ML-based efficiency calculations on the factory floor.

Factory acceptance delivery

Drove a software logic update to dynamically reset shortest-path parameters — stabilizing packet transmission and restoring production data reliability.

Challenges Tackled

Integration blockers surfaced during on-site validation and cross-team rollout.

Intermittent zero-data blocking analytics

Packet loss appeared sporadically under production conditions — blocking real-time voltage and power analysis until the root cause in routing logic was isolated and fixed.

Shortest-path routing under load

Increasing node counts during stress tests exposed algorithm limits — requiring dynamic parameter resets rather than static routing assumptions.

US–Japan coordination across time zones

Test-cycle misunderstandings and delayed feedback loops slowed acceptance — resolved through structured bilingual communication and aligned validation criteria.

Key Contributions

  • Delivered successful on-site deployment — aligned US startup and Japan stakeholders on test criteria and closed the loop from failure diagnosis to factory acceptance
  • Designed stress test cycles (increasing node counts) to isolate routing failures and identify limits in the shortest-path algorithm
  • Owned investigation of intermittent zero-data transmission issues that blocked downstream analytics and ML-based efficiency calculations
  • Drove a software logic update to dynamically reset shortest-path parameters, stabilizing packet transmission and restoring data reliability
  • Served as primary EN–JP coordination point between US and Japan teams, reducing test-cycle misunderstandings and accelerating product acceptance
